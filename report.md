## Appendix

### Methodology

1. **Scoring System**

   Opportunities were evaluated on three dimensions, each scored from 1.0 to 10.0, then combined into a **Total Score** (simple average) used for prioritization:

   - **Feasibility** – How realistically the opportunity can be pursued, given technical capabilities, required partnerships, capital intensity, infrastructure, and execution risk.
   - **Impact** – Potential financial, strategic, and reputational upside, including award size, policy significance, and prospects for follow-on work.
   - **Alignment** – Fit with strategic focus areas such as critical minerals and materials, industrial decarbonization, advanced manufacturing, energy systems, and defense-oriented AI/ML.

   Priority bands:
   - **High Priority:** Total Score ≥ 8.0
   - **Medium Priority:** 6.0 ≤ Total Score ≤ 7.9
   - **Low Priority:** Total Score < 6.0

2. **Workflow Phases**

   1. **Discovery:** Domain-focused agents scanned federal sources (DOE, DoD, DARPA, NSF, NETL, etc.) and news streams to identify recent and evergreen opportunities in the target domains.
   2. **Aggregation:** An aggregation step merged duplicate findings, standardized metadata (title, source, sector, dates, URLs), and produced **enriched descriptions** that contextualize each opportunity in terms of technology, sector, and strategic relevance.
   3. **Scoring:** Each consolidated opportunity was rated along Feasibility, Impact, and Alignment with accompanying written justifications to make the reasoning transparent and repeatable.
   4. **Reporting:** This report structures the scored opportunities into High/Medium/Low priority groups, adds decision-focused narratives, and formats content into a professional, scannable Markdown document ready for decision-makers.

3. **Data Quality and Enrichment**

   - **Sources:** Data was drawn from authoritative federal websites and contract announcements. Where specific dates or granular details were not available, readers are directed to the source URLs (e.g., DOE/NSF/NETL portals) for the most current information.
   - **Enrichment:** Descriptions in this report leverage the aggregation phase’s enriched text, ensuring that each opportunity is presented with sufficient context (policy objectives, technology focus, sector relevance) for strategic decision-making.
   - **Handling Missing Information:**
     - Missing dates are labeled as **“See source for details”** or **“Not specified”**.
     - Missing URLs are addressed as **“See source documentation”** or **“Contact source”** so that the report remains professional and complete without placeholders like “TBD” or “TODO”.

This methodology ensures that every opportunity—whether immediately actionable or not—is captured with clear reasoning, enabling leadership to decide **what to pursue now, what to monitor, and where to invest in capability or partnership development** for future calls.
